STAN Estimation

Bo Werth

2015-04-01

General

STAN combines time series from National Accounts by economic activity. The estimation methodology consists of three major components:

  • link series using growth rates
  • increase detail by appling shares from secondary sources to aggregates of the main data source
  • top-down adjustment to ensure consistency on all levels of the industry hierarchy

Data Requirements

The data needs to meet the following requirements:

Data for estimation

  • variables in columns: cou, var, ind, year, value, sou (order does not matter)

Extend

If the time coverage of our primary data source is insufficient and a secondary source covers for example more recent years, we attempt to extend the main source using the annual growth rate of the secondary source for years missing in the main source.

\[ estim_{t+1} = main_{t} * \frac{sec_{t+1}}{sec_{t}} \]

In the example below, the sources are as follows:

  • Primary source: STANandBTDi3
  • Secondary source: STANandBTDi4
  • Estimated source: NAPATCH

The dataset structure for this example looks as follows:

cou var ind year value sou
AUT VALU CTOTAL 1995 2.146e+11 STANandBTDi3
AUT VALU CTOTAL 1996 2.095e+11 STANandBTDi3
AUT VALU CTOTAL 1997 1.859e+11 STANandBTDi3
AUT VALU CTOTAL 1998 1.908e+11 STANandBTDi3
AUT VALU CTOTAL 1999 1.891e+11 STANandBTDi3
AUT VALU CTOTAL 2000 1.719e+11 STANandBTDi3

Apply distribution from secondary sources

In case our data primary source is not sufficiently detailed and additional detail is available in a secondary source, we attempt to apply the distribution from the secondary source to the primary source.

In the example below, the sources are as follows:

  • Primary source: STANandBTDi3
  • Secondary source: INDSTAT32
  • Estimation results: NAPATCH

In this example we observe two oddities:

  • the primary sources stops in 2009, therefore the estimated results don’t extend beyond
  • the secondary source contains zeros in 2008-2010 and this is transferred to the estimated results
